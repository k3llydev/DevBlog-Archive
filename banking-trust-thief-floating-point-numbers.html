What is a "floating-point" number?

In computer science, floating-point refers to a method of "storing" real numbers in computer-based arithmetic to support trade-offs between range and precision. Most typical computer systems use the Double Precision IEEE 754 standard, but there are some variants:

Since our main topic involves JavaScript it's important to mention that as stated by the ECMAScript standard, JavaScript also follows the IEEE 754 standard. This means JavaScript uses a double-precision floating-point decimals storing method and this will help us understand later on the "limits" of it.
